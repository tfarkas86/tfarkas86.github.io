Book Review: Blown To Bits

27 April 2022

Review

For the final single credit of my Statistics degree at the University of New Mexico, I decided to take a half-semester course in the Computer Science department, Social and Ethical Issues in Computing.

The main requirement of the course is to read Blown To Bits: Your Life, Liberty, and Happiness After the Digital Explosion, by Hal Abelson et al. (2021, 2nd Ed.), and it's turned out to be a real treat in many ways.

The book deftly walks the line between textbook and popular science. It serves as an ideal summary of societally impactful computing trends of the past 100 years, weaving techological, legal, and societal history, technical details, and ethical considerations into a compelling narrative.
